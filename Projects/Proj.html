Breaking the Benchmark: Revealing LLM Bias via Minimal Contextual Augmentation ✖ 
Investigated whether large language models truly reduce social bias by applying minimal, meaning-preserving augmentations to the BBQ benchmark, extending evaluation to underexplored categories like ageism and disability, revealing latent bias patterns and model-specific robustness differences.

Paths to Policies: Offline Coverage Complexity from Combinatorial Bandits to RL (In progress) ✖ 
We study how sequential problem representation changes offline learning difficulty by comparing shortest-path bandit, semi-bandit, and MDP formulations under identical behavior data. Our framework characterizes when local decomposition improves statistical efficiency and when state-occupancy mismatch becomes the dominant statistical bottleneck.

RADAR: Risk-Aware Dynamic Adaptive Returns ✖ 
RADAR learns per-stock regularized Sharpe q-scores, balancing exploration via trade counts using UCB-RSSR; ensures anytime average-cost compliance for observation-agnostic, capital-efficient portfolio adaptation, risk-managed, profitable growth.
